Spaghetti Bolognese

Decription

Indulge in the comfort of Spaghetti Bolognese, where perfectly cooked spaghetti is lavishly coated in a savory, slow-cooked beef and tomato sauce, infused with herbs and a hint of red wine, bringing a symphony of flavors to this beloved Italian classic.

Ingredients

  • Spaghetti pasta
  • Ground beef
  • Onion, chopped
  • Garlic, finely chopped
  • Canned tomatoes, diced
  • Tomato Pureé
  • Salt and Pepper
  • Parmesan cheese, grated

Steps

  1. Cook the Pasta: Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Add the spaghetti and cook according to the package instructions until al dente. Drain and set aside.
  2. Brown the Beef: In a large skillet or saucepan, heat a little oil over medium heat. Add the ground beef and cook, stirring and breaking it up with a spoon, until it's no longer pink. Remove the beef from the pan and set aside.
  3. Sauté the Vegetables: In the same pan, add the chopped onion and minced garlic. Cook over medium heat until the onion is soft and translucent.
  4. Combine Beef and Vegetables: Return the cooked beef to the pan with the onions and garlic.
  5. Add Tomatoes: Stir in the canned tomatoes and tomato paste. Mix well to combine.
  6. Season the Sauce: Season with salt and pepper. You can also add a pinch of dried herbs like basil or oregano if you have them.
  7. Simmer the Sauce: Reduce the heat to low and let the sauce simmer gently for about 15 minutes, stirring occasionally. If the sauce is too thick, add a little water.
  8. Combine Pasta and Sauce: Add the cooked spaghetti to the sauce, tossing to coat the pasta evenly. Alternatively, you can serve the sauce over the pasta.
  9. Check for Seasoning: Taste and adjust the seasoning with more salt and pepper if needed.
  10. Serve: Serve hot, garnished with grated Parmesan cheese if desired.
